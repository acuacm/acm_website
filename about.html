About

ACM's goal is to build a foundation for students to participate in the technology community in meaningful ways. We offer students opportunities to learn, create, compete, and be inspired.

ACM also organizes special events like LAN parties and programming contests.

Time: 7pm
Location: MBB 316

Learn

We offer to teach anything tech related in preparation to work on club projects.

We learn everything from web development to C++ and Python to robotics. Lessons on these will be given periodically during chapter meetings, and participation is encouraged.


Create

After learning a new language or system we will try to complete a project using those skills.

We will usually break into teams and either each team works on a certain part of the project, or each team works to produce the same end product, allowing us to use the best. Currently we have no completed projects. In the future we will mention finished ones here.

Compete

At the end of each semester we host a programming contest and give out cool prizes like Raspberry Pi's.

The contests are meant to be low-stress competitions to see if you're able to think under a clock. Sign up with a friend or get paired and try it. We will announce the time and place once everything is set up.

Placing in an official programming contest is worth putting on your resume. ACM's contests are meant to help practice for the larger contests. Try one out even if you aren't interested in competing locally or nationally. You may win a prize.

Be Inspired

About every month we invite a speaker from the tech field to talk about their job and what they do.

Many computer science professionals work in other areas as well; bible text, biology, air force, tech support, and quantum mechanics, etc. By learning about what they do we hope to inspire you to look into other fields and the possibilities of programming.
